The Philosophy of Our Practice

We are committed to ensure that women living in this part of Hungary also have the option to give birth with a midwife, in an out-of-hospital setting. We are striving to support each woman and family in finding the way which best suits their values, cultural preferences and lifestyle choices. Our services include care all throughout the childbearing year – and beyond. Our care is based on the principles of shared decision-making, mutual trust and open dialogue.

Prenatal Care 

After making the initial contact, we meet our prospective clients in the office, where we discuss the needs and expectations of the mother and her support team. During prenatal care, our practice cooperates with the other care providers involved in your care, and we make referrals as necessary.
Based on your individual needs, we together develop a care plan. Our usual schedule includes the following visits, each visit lasting about an hour:
• Once a month until 30 weeks gestation
• Once every 2 weeks 36 weeks gestation
• Weekly after 36 weeks gestation
• 2-3 times a week or as needed after 40 weeks gestation
• A visit in your home or other, planned location of the birth (around week 36) 


SUPPORT DURING LABOR, BIRTH AND THE IMMEDIATE POSTPARTUM
We provide labor and birth support in our birth center or in the home of the client. Two midwives are present during your labor, providing one-to-one labor support, based on your individual wishes and preferences. We are skilled and equipped to use gentle pain management and natural labor support techniques, including aromatherapy,massage and wet compresses, acupuncture, rebozo, hydrotherapy or the use of TENS. Our midwives are usually present for 3 hours after the baby is born, and midwifery care includes a complete newborn exam. You can of course invite other people to support your labor, whether they are friends, relatives, a doula or a birth photographer.

POSPARTUM CARE
We help you to prepare for the phyisiological and emotional changes of the postpartum period during prenatal care, so that this transition can happen as smoothly as possible. We also support you and your family during this 6-week period with home visits and office visits. During this period we provide information on the care of the newborn, we are available for consultation on breastfeeding and for any other issues regarding your physical and emotional recovery.

WHO CAN GIVE BIRTH AT HOME?

Our practice is operating according to the rules set by the relevant Hungarian legislation in effect. The requirements of this regulation can be found here (link). Please read it carefully. As relevant legislation specifies the conditions when a homebirth or midwifery care is not allowed, please consult this list to see if you have any of these conditions, and to make sure that you understand that there might be circumstances when a change in the plan of care becomes necessary due to this regulation. We will try our best to help and support you in finding other care provider(s) and health care options fitting your needs under the given circumstances.

ADVOCACY, ACTIVISM
After the birth of my second daughter I got deeply involved in birth rights activism, which is still an important part of my work. I was contributing to the drafting of the current homebirth regulation, and with many others, we actively lobbied on the national and the international level for enhancing the scope of competence of midwives, in accordance with the relevant directive of the European Union. This activism lead to the regulation that now allows midwifery prenatal care.
I am a member of the Association of Homebirth Midwives, and an activist in the Improved Maternity Care! movement since its foundation. In both of my roles I am working for higher standards and evidence-based maternity services in all settings and by all care-providers.

EDUCATION
I had enrolled to the Midwives College of Utah, in the US, just before my third child was born, in 2013, and graduated in 2018.
My education prepared me to become an autonomous and accountable health professional, committed to follow evidence-based practices and serve women with my best knowledge.
I joined A Te Szülésed Midwifery Practice as a student midwife in 2015. During my apprenticeship I attended over 50 births, in the birth center, in the home of the clients, and also births as a doula in hospitals. 

OTHER INTERESTS
I have a degree an economy and environmental protection as well. Before becoming a midwife I worked as a project manager in nature conservation projects, in Hungary and in many European countries. Preserving our natural values and making our world sustainable is still a passion for me.
My family is farming, and as a kind of nature-friendly grassland management, we keep grazing animals (sheep, cattle and horses). I like gardening and have a manufacture of herbal products.
